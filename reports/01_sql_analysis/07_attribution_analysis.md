# Attribution Analysis Report

## Objective

The objective of this analysis is to evaluate the effectiveness of marketing acquisition channels by examining their contribution to website traffic, orders, revenue, and conversion performance.

Unlike Acquisition Analysis, which focuses on traffic generation, Attribution Analysis focuses on the business value created by each traffic source.

---

# 1. Traffic Attribution

## Results

| Source         | Sessions | Traffic Share |
| -------------- | -------: | ------------: |
| gsearch        |  316,035 |        66.83% |
| organic/direct |   83,328 |        17.62% |
| bsearch        |   62,823 |        13.29% |
| socialbook     |   10,685 |         2.26% |

## Findings

Website traffic is heavily concentrated in a single acquisition source.

gsearch contributes nearly two-thirds of all website sessions, making it the primary traffic acquisition channel. Organic/direct traffic and bsearch provide secondary traffic streams, while socialbook contributes only a small portion of overall traffic.

## Business Implications

* Business growth is strongly dependent on gsearch traffic.
* Traffic diversification may reduce acquisition risk.
* Alternative acquisition channels could provide opportunities for future growth.

---

# 2. Source Efficiency

## Results

| Source         | Sessions | Orders |       Revenue | Conversion Rate |
| -------------- | -------: | -----: | ------------: | --------------: |
| gsearch        |  316,035 | 21,333 | $1,276,144.89 |           6.75% |
| organic/direct |   83,328 |  6,118 |   $371,433.03 |           7.34% |
| bsearch        |   62,823 |  4,519 |   $268,672.50 |           7.19% |
| socialbook     |   10,685 |    343 |    $22,259.33 |           3.21% |

## Findings

### gsearch is the Primary Growth Engine

gsearch generates the largest number of sessions, orders, and revenue.

The channel contributes approximately:

* 66.8% of website traffic
* 66.0% of total revenue

This demonstrates that gsearch is the dominant acquisition channel and the primary driver of business growth.

---

### organic/direct Traffic is the Most Efficient Source

Although organic/direct traffic generates significantly fewer sessions than gsearch, it achieves the highest conversion rate at 7.34%.

This suggests that users who arrive through direct visits or organic discovery exhibit stronger purchase intent and are more likely to complete a transaction.

---

### bsearch Delivers Consistent Performance

bsearch generates meaningful traffic volume while maintaining a conversion rate similar to organic/direct traffic.

Its performance indicates that it serves as a valuable secondary acquisition channel and contributes positively to both revenue generation and conversion performance.

---

### socialbook Underperforms Relative to Other Sources

socialbook records the lowest conversion rate at 3.21%, less than half the conversion rate achieved by organic/direct traffic.

Although the channel contributes some traffic volume, its ability to generate orders and revenue is substantially weaker than other acquisition sources.

## Business Implications

* Continue investing in gsearch as the primary growth channel.
* Strengthen organic acquisition efforts due to their high conversion efficiency.
* Maintain bsearch as a supporting acquisition channel.
* Reassess socialbook targeting, audience quality, or campaign strategy.

---

# Key Insights

1. gsearch contributes approximately 67% of total website traffic.
2. gsearch generates approximately $1.28 million in revenue and remains the primary business growth driver.
3. organic/direct traffic achieves the highest conversion rate (7.34%).
4. Users arriving through organic/direct channels demonstrate stronger purchase intent than visitors from paid social channels.
5. bsearch performs consistently and serves as an effective secondary acquisition channel.
6. socialbook delivers the weakest conversion performance across all traffic sources.
7. Business growth remains highly dependent on gsearch traffic.
8. Diversifying acquisition channels may reduce long-term marketing risk.
9. Marketing effectiveness varies significantly across acquisition sources.
10. Traffic volume alone does not determine business value; conversion efficiency is equally important.

---

# Conclusion

The analysis shows that gsearch is the dominant driver of traffic, orders, and revenue, making it the most important acquisition channel for the business. However, organic/direct traffic demonstrates superior conversion efficiency, indicating higher customer intent and stronger traffic quality.

While bsearch provides reliable supporting performance, socialbook currently underperforms and may require optimization or strategic review. Overall, future growth opportunities lie not only in acquiring more traffic but also in improving the quality and efficiency of traffic sources.
