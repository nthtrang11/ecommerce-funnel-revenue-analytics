# Retention Analysis Report

## Objective

The objective of this analysis is to evaluate customer retention behavior, compare the performance of new and returning visitors, and assess the business impact of repeat customers on conversion and revenue generation.

---

# 1. Customer Mix

## Results

| Customer Type | Sessions |
| ------------- | -------: |
| New           |  394,318 |
| Repeat        |   78,553 |

## Findings

The majority of website traffic comes from new visitors.

Repeat sessions account for approximately 16.6% of total sessions, while new visitors generate over 83% of website traffic.

This indicates that business growth is currently driven primarily by customer acquisition rather than customer retention.

## Business Implications

* The business relies heavily on attracting new visitors.
* Improving retention could reduce dependence on paid acquisition channels.
* Repeat customer growth represents a potential opportunity for sustainable long-term revenue.

---

# 2. Conversion Performance by Customer Type

## Results

| Customer Type | Sessions | Orders | Conversion Rate |
| ------------- | -------: | -----: | --------------: |
| New           |  394,318 | 26,164 |           6.64% |
| Repeat        |   78,553 |  6,149 |           7.83% |

## Findings

Repeat visitors convert at a higher rate than new visitors.

The conversion rate for repeat customers is 7.83%, compared to 6.64% for new customers.

This represents an approximately 18% improvement in conversion performance.

The results suggest that customers who have previously interacted with the website are more likely to complete a purchase.

## Business Implications

* Returning visitors represent a higher-quality customer segment.
* Retention initiatives may generate stronger conversion outcomes than acquisition-focused campaigns alone.
* Marketing efforts targeting existing customers could produce higher returns on investment.

---

# 3. Revenue by Customer Type

## Results

| Customer Type | Orders |       Revenue |    AOV |
| ------------- | -----: | ------------: | -----: |
| New           | 26,164 | $1,566,274.92 | $59.86 |
| Repeat        |  6,149 |   $372,234.83 | $60.54 |

## Findings

New customers generate the majority of total revenue, contributing approximately $1.57 million.

Repeat customers generate $372,234.83 in revenue, accounting for a smaller share of total revenue due to their lower traffic volume.

Average order value is nearly identical across customer groups, suggesting that repeat customers do not spend substantially more per order than first-time visitors.

## Business Implications

* Revenue differences are primarily driven by customer volume rather than spending behavior.
* Growth opportunities may come from increasing the number of repeat customers rather than attempting to increase order value.

---

# 4. Customer Engagement

## Results

| Metric                    | Value |
| ------------------------- | ----: |
| Average Sessions per User |  1.20 |

## Findings

The average user visits the website approximately 1.2 times.

This indicates that most visitors interact with the website only once and do not return frequently.

The result suggests relatively weak retention performance and highlights a dependence on continuous customer acquisition.

## Business Implications

* Customer retention remains a significant growth opportunity.
* The business may benefit from loyalty programs, remarketing campaigns, email marketing, and customer engagement initiatives.
* Increasing repeat visitation could improve both conversion efficiency and revenue stability.

---

# 5. Revenue Share by Customer Type

## Results

| Customer Type | Revenue Share |
| ------------- | ------------: |
| New           |         80.8% |
| Repeat        |         19.2% |

## Findings

Repeat customers generate 19.2% of total revenue while representing only 16.6% of total sessions.

This indicates that repeat visitors contribute disproportionately more revenue than their traffic share would suggest.

Although repeat customers represent a relatively small segment of website traffic, they create greater business value on a per-session basis.

## Hypothesis Evaluation

### H10 — Repeat Customers Generate Disproportionate Revenue

**Supported**

Repeat customers account for a larger share of revenue than their share of sessions, demonstrating above-average economic value.

## Business Implications

* Retention investments may produce stronger long-term returns than acquisition alone.
* Increasing repeat customer share could improve revenue efficiency without requiring proportional increases in traffic acquisition spending.

---

# Key Insights

1. New visitors account for approximately 83% of website traffic.
2. Repeat visitors represent only 16.6% of sessions, indicating a relatively low retention level.
3. Repeat customers convert significantly better than new visitors (7.83% vs 6.64%).
4. Average order value is nearly identical between customer groups.
5. Repeat customers do not spend substantially more per order, but they purchase more efficiently.
6. The average user visits the website only 1.2 times, suggesting considerable room for retention improvement.
7. Repeat customers contribute 19.2% of revenue despite representing only 16.6% of sessions.
8. Repeat customers generate higher business value per session than new visitors.
9. H10 is supported by the data.
10. Retention improvement represents a meaningful opportunity for sustainable business growth.

---

# Conclusion

The business currently relies heavily on acquiring new visitors to drive traffic and revenue. However, repeat customers demonstrate stronger conversion performance and contribute disproportionately more revenue relative to their traffic share.

While overall retention remains relatively weak, the performance of returning customers suggests that investments in customer retention, loyalty, and re-engagement initiatives could significantly improve long-term business efficiency and revenue sustainability.
