# Revenue Analysis Report

## Objective

The objective of this analysis is to evaluate revenue performance across acquisition channels, marketing campaigns, devices, and products in order to identify the primary drivers of business growth and profitability.

---

# 1. Revenue Overview

## Results

| Metric                    |         Value |
| ------------------------- | ------------: |
| Orders                    |        32,313 |
| Revenue                   | $1,938,509.75 |
| Average Order Value (AOV) |        $59.99 |

## Findings

The business generated $1.94 million in revenue from 32,313 completed orders.

The average order value is approximately $60 per order, providing a baseline for comparing customer spending behavior across channels, campaigns, and devices.

---

# 2. Revenue by Acquisition Source

## Results

| Source         | Orders |       Revenue |    AOV |
| -------------- | -----: | ------------: | -----: |
| gsearch        | 21,333 | $1,276,144.89 | $59.82 |
| organic/direct |  6,118 |   $371,433.03 | $60.71 |
| bsearch        |  4,519 |   $268,672.50 | $59.45 |
| socialbook     |    343 |    $22,259.33 | $64.90 |

## Findings

Revenue contribution varies substantially across acquisition sources.

**gsearch** is the dominant revenue driver, generating approximately 66% of total website revenue. Organic and direct traffic represent the second-largest revenue source, while socialbook contributes only a small portion of overall revenue.

Average order values remain relatively consistent across sources, indicating that revenue differences are primarily driven by order volume rather than customer spending per order.

## Hypothesis Evaluation

### H6 — Revenue Contribution Differs Across Acquisition Sources

**Supported**

Revenue generation is highly concentrated among acquisition channels, with gsearch contributing the majority of total revenue.

## Business Implications

* Continue investing in high-performing acquisition channels.
* Evaluate opportunities to scale revenue from organic and direct traffic.
* Assess whether low-performing channels justify ongoing marketing investment.

# 3. Revenue by Campaign

## Results

| Campaign         | Orders |       Revenue |    AOV |
| ---------------- | -----: | ------------: | -----: |
| nonbrand         | 22,640 | $1,349,977.69 | $59.63 |
| brand            |  3,212 |   $194,839.70 | $60.66 |
| desktop_targeted |    288 |    $18,516.10 | $64.29 |
| pilot            |     55 |     $3,743.23 | $68.06 |

## Findings

The nonbrand campaign generates the majority of business revenue, contributing approximately 70% of total revenue.

Although some smaller campaigns achieve slightly higher average order values, their impact on overall business performance remains limited due to significantly lower order volume.

These results suggest that campaign scale is a more important revenue driver than differences in customer spending behavior.

## Business Implications

* Continue prioritizing high-volume campaigns that consistently generate revenue.
* Investigate whether successful campaign strategies can be replicated across other marketing initiatives.
* Monitor campaign efficiency alongside revenue performance.

# 4. Revenue by Device Type

## Results

| Device Type | Orders |       Revenue |    AOV |
| ----------- | -----: | ------------: | -----: |
| Desktop     | 27,805 | $1,665,757.84 | $59.91 |
| Mobile      |  4,508 |   $272,751.91 | $60.50 |

## Findings

Desktop users generate the vast majority of revenue, contributing approximately 86% of total website revenue.

However, average order values are nearly identical across device types. Mobile users spend slightly more per order on average, but the difference is minimal.

The revenue gap between devices is therefore driven primarily by conversion performance rather than customer spending behavior.

## Hypothesis Evaluation

### H7 — Average Order Value Differs Across Device Types

**Inconclusive**

The observed difference in average order value between desktop and mobile users is small and cannot be considered meaningful without statistical validation.

Further testing will be performed during the Statistical Validation phase.

## Business Implications

* Revenue growth opportunities on mobile are likely related to conversion improvements rather than pricing or basket-size optimization.
* Mobile experience optimization should remain a strategic priority.

# 5. Product Revenue Analysis

## Results

| Product                    | Items Sold |       Revenue |
| -------------------------- | ---------: | ------------: |
| The Original Mr. Fuzzy     |     24,226 | $1,211,057.74 |
| The Forever Love Bear      |      5,796 |   $347,702.04 |
| The Birthday Sugar Panda   |      4,985 |   $229,260.15 |
| The Hudson River Mini Bear |      5,018 |   $150,489.82 |

## Findings

Revenue is heavily concentrated in a single product.

The Original Mr. Fuzzy generates approximately 62% of total company revenue and significantly outperforms all other products.

This concentration creates both an opportunity and a risk. While the product serves as the primary growth driver, business performance is highly dependent on its continued success.

## Business Implications

* Protect and optimize the performance of The Original Mr. Fuzzy.
* Explore opportunities to increase revenue contribution from secondary products.
* Reduce business dependence on a single revenue source.

# 6. Profitability Analysis

## Results

| Product                    |       Revenue |        COGS | Gross Profit |
| -------------------------- | ------------: | ----------: | -----------: |
| The Original Mr. Fuzzy     | $1,211,057.74 | $472,164.74 |  $738,893.00 |
| The Forever Love Bear      |   $347,702.04 | $130,352.04 |  $217,350.00 |
| The Birthday Sugar Panda   |   $229,260.15 |  $72,232.65 |  $157,027.50 |
| The Hudson River Mini Bear |   $150,489.82 |  $47,620.82 |  $102,869.00 |

## Findings

Profitability patterns closely mirror revenue performance.

The Original Mr. Fuzzy is both the highest-revenue and highest-profit product, contributing nearly three-quarters of a million dollars in gross profit.

No product appears to generate high revenue while producing disproportionately low profit, indicating a relatively healthy product portfolio from a margin perspective.

## Business Implications

* Continue supporting products that generate both strong revenue and strong profitability.
* Use gross profit alongside revenue when evaluating future product investments.
* Monitor product mix to ensure sustainable profitability growth.
