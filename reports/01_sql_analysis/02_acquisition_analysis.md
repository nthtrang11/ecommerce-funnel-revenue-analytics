# Acquisition Analysis

## Objective

The objective of this analysis is to evaluate traffic acquisition performance across marketing channels and campaigns.

The analysis focuses on answering the following business questions:

1. Which acquisition sources generate the most website traffic?
2. Which acquisition sources generate the most orders?
3. Which acquisition sources deliver the highest-quality traffic?
4. Which marketing campaigns perform most effectively?
5. Which acquisition sources generate the most loyal customers?

The analysis supports Hypotheses H1, H2, and H3 defined in the project hypothesis framework.

---

# Dataset

Tables used:

* website_sessions
* orders

Metrics analyzed:

* Sessions
* Orders
* Conversion Rate
* Repeat Sessions
* Repeat Session Rate

---

# Key Findings

## 1. Gsearch Is the Primary Traffic Driver

| Source         | Sessions |
| -------------- | -------: |
| gsearch        |  316,035 |
| organic/direct |   83,328 |
| bsearch        |   62,823 |
| socialbook     |   10,685 |

Gsearch generated 316,035 sessions and accounted for approximately 67% of total website traffic.

This indicates that the business relies heavily on Google Search as its primary acquisition channel.

### Business Implication

A significant portion of website traffic depends on Google Search performance. Any decline in search visibility or paid search efficiency could have a substantial impact on overall traffic volume.

---

## 2. Gsearch Generates the Highest Number of Orders

| Source         | Orders |
| -------------- | -----: |
| gsearch        | 21,333 |
| organic/direct |  6,118 |
| bsearch        |  4,519 |
| socialbook     |    343 |

Gsearch generated the largest number of customer orders, producing more than three times the orders generated by organic/direct traffic.

### Business Implication

Google Search serves as the primary revenue-generating acquisition channel and remains critical to business growth.

---

## 3. Traffic Quality Differs Across Acquisition Sources

| Source         | Orders | Sessions | Conversion Rate |
| -------------- | -----: | -------: | --------------: |
| organic/direct |  6,118 |   83,328 |           7.34% |
| bsearch        |  4,519 |   62,823 |           7.19% |
| gsearch        | 21,333 |  316,035 |           6.75% |
| socialbook     |    343 |   10,685 |           3.21% |

Organic/direct traffic achieved the highest conversion rate at 7.34%, followed closely by bsearch at 7.19%.

Although gsearch generated the highest traffic volume, its conversion rate was lower than both organic/direct and bsearch traffic.

Socialbook produced the lowest conversion rate at 3.21%.

### Business Implication

Traffic volume alone is not an indicator of acquisition quality. Organic and direct visitors demonstrate stronger purchase intent and convert more efficiently than paid traffic sources.

---

## 4. Brand Campaigns Outperform Nonbrand Campaigns

| Campaign         | Orders | Sessions | Conversion Rate |
| ---------------- | -----: | -------: | --------------: |
| brand            |  3,212 |   41,243 |           7.79% |
| nonbrand         | 22,640 |  337,615 |           6.71% |
| desktop_targeted |    288 |    5,590 |           5.15% |
| pilot            |     55 |    5,095 |           1.08% |

Brand campaigns achieved the highest conversion rate at 7.79%.

Nonbrand campaigns generated the majority of traffic and orders but converted less efficiently.

The pilot campaign significantly underperformed all other campaigns.

### Business Implication

Brand campaigns appear to attract users with stronger purchase intent, while nonbrand campaigns primarily support traffic acquisition and market expansion.

The pilot campaign requires further investigation to determine whether targeting, messaging, or landing page issues contributed to its low performance.

---

## 5. Organic/Direct Traffic Generates the Most Loyal Customers

| Source         | Total Sessions | Repeat Sessions | Repeat Session Rate |
| -------------- | -------------: | --------------: | ------------------: |
| organic/direct |         83,328 |          52,329 |              62.80% |
| bsearch        |         62,823 |           5,021 |               7.99% |
| gsearch        |        316,035 |          21,203 |               6.71% |
| socialbook     |         10,685 |               0 |               0.00% |

Organic/direct traffic achieved a repeat session rate of 62.8%, substantially higher than all other acquisition channels.

Bsearch and gsearch generated similar repeat session rates, while socialbook generated no repeat traffic.

### Business Implication

Organic and direct visitors demonstrate significantly stronger customer loyalty and long-term engagement than paid acquisition channels.

Investments in brand awareness and customer retention may create greater long-term value than traffic acquisition alone.

---

# Hypothesis Evaluation

## H1 — Traffic Quality Differs Across Acquisition Sources

Status: Supported

Conversion rates vary substantially across acquisition channels, ranging from 3.21% to 7.34%.

---

## H2 — Acquisition Sources Differ in Their Ability to Generate Loyal Customers

Status: Supported

Repeat session rates vary significantly across traffic sources, with organic/direct traffic generating substantially higher customer loyalty.

---

## H3 — Marketing Campaign Performance Varies Significantly

Status: Supported

Campaign conversion rates range from 1.08% to 7.79%, indicating substantial differences in campaign effectiveness.

---

# Strategic Recommendations

## 1. Maintain Investment in Google Search

Google Search remains the primary source of both traffic and orders and should continue to be a core acquisition channel.

---

## 2. Strengthen Brand-Building Initiatives

Organic and direct traffic generate the highest conversion rates and strongest customer loyalty. Increasing brand awareness may improve both acquisition efficiency and long-term customer value.

---

## 3. Optimize Socialbook Performance

Socialbook demonstrates significantly lower conversion performance than other channels. Further investigation should be conducted to evaluate audience targeting, creative effectiveness, and landing page experience.

---

## 4. Review Pilot Campaign Strategy

The pilot campaign achieved the lowest conversion rate among all campaigns. Campaign objectives, targeting parameters, and execution strategy should be reassessed before additional investment.

---

# Conclusion

Google Search is the dominant acquisition channel in terms of both traffic and orders. However, organic/direct traffic delivers the highest conversion rates and strongest customer loyalty.

The analysis demonstrates that acquisition channels differ substantially in traffic quality, customer retention, and campaign effectiveness. These findings support all acquisition-related hypotheses and provide a foundation for subsequent conversion, revenue, retention, and attribution analyses.
